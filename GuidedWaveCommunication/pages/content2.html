Numerical Aperture Experiment Report

Aim

The objective of this experiment is to estimate the Numerical Aperture of a 1 mm diameter plastic fibre at 632 nm.

Materials Required

  • Optical Fibre
  • He-Ne 632 nm Laser
  • Numerical Aperture Measurement Unit

Theory

Numerical Aperture (NA) of a fibre is a measure of the acceptance angle of light in the fibre. Light launched at angles greater than this maximum acceptance angle does not get coupled to propagating modes in the fibre and does not reach the receiver at the other end. The Numerical Aperture is useful for computing optical power coupling between an optical source and fibre, from fibre to photodetector, and between two fibres.

Procedure

  1. Setup the experiment as shown in the figure.
  2. Ensure that the cut planes of the optical fibre are perpendicular to its axis.
  3. Insert one end of the fibre into the Numerical Aperture measurement unit.
  4. Adjust the fibre such that its tip is from the screen and ensure the cut end is parallel to the measurement unit.
  5. Gently tighten the screw to hold the fibre in place.
  6. Connect the other end of the fibre to the laser, ensuring light emerges at the other end.
  7. Measure the diameter of the spot size in both directions.
  8. Change the height (d) of the fibre from the measurement unit and repeat the measurements.
