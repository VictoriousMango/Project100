Experiment 6: Laser Beam Waist Characterization by Knife Edge Technique

Aim

To characterize the waist of a laser beam using the Knife Edge Technique.

Apparatus

The equipment and materials required for the experiment are:

  • Laser Source
  • Knife Edge
  • Translation Stage
  • Photodetector
  • Power Meter
  • Beam Profile Analyzer (optional)

Theory

The waist of a laser beam is the region where the beam diameter is at its minimum, and the intensity profile follows a Gaussian distribution. The Knife Edge Technique is a method used to measure the beam waist by partially blocking the beam and recording the power as a function of the knife edge position.

By plotting the power variation and fitting the data to a Gaussian function, the beam waist (ω0) and other parameters such as the Rayleigh range can be determined. This technique provides a precise measurement of the laser beam's spatial characteristics.

Procedure

Follow these steps to perform the experiment:

  1. Set up the laser source on an optical table, ensuring proper alignment of the beam.
  2. Place the knife edge on a translation stage and position it perpendicular to the laser beam.
  3. Place the photodetector behind the knife edge to measure the transmitted power.
  4. Move the knife edge incrementally across the beam using the translation stage, and record the corresponding power readings using the power meter.
  5. Plot the measured power as a function of the knife edge position.
  6. Fit the data to a Gaussian function to determine the beam waist (ω0) and other beam parameters.

Observations

Record the following observations:

  • Knife edge positions and corresponding power readings.
  • Gaussian fit of the power data.
  • Calculated beam waist (ω0), Rayleigh range, and beam divergence.

Conclusion/Results

The experiment successfully characterized the laser beam waist using the Knife Edge Technique. The beam waist (ω0) and other parameters such as the Rayleigh range were calculated, demonstrating the Gaussian nature of the laser beam profile.
